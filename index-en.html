LifeGuard AI SmartHealth Watch

Real-time health monitoring and emergency alert system.

LifeGuard AI is a GNSS-enabled smart health watch concept that tracks live health data and automatically detects emergency situations. It analyzes critical risks such as heart rate anomalies, fever, movement irregularities and falls, aiming to instantly inform the right person about who, where and what in an emergency.

It is an AI-assisted personal safety solution that can be used in hospitals, factories, field teams, sports facilities or family tracking systems.

  • GNSS + real-time location tracking
  • Heart rate / SpO₂ / body temperature monitoring
  • Fall and fainting detection
  • Data transfer to the mobile app via Bluetooth Low Energy
  • Indoor / outdoor emergency notification (SOS)
  • 40 mm case optimized for daily use

Summary

What does the watch do, and who is it for?

The cards below explain the expected benefits for hospitals, safety teams, field workers and families, without going deep into sensor-level details.

For healthcare providers

Heart rate, SpO₂, temperature and movement data help clinical teams closely monitor high-risk patients. In case of falls, fainting or sudden changes, questions like who the patient is, where they are and what their status is can be answered within seconds.

For occupational safety

In factories, construction sites or field operations, workers’ location and status can be monitored from a central platform. In accidents, fainting or heat stress, incident logs and response times become more traceable and reportable.

For field teams

The location and condition of maintenance, security or emergency response teams in the field can be tracked. GNSS data and incident logs can be used for both operational efficiency and risk management.

For families / individual use

By tracking the user’s heart rate, SpO₂, temperature and movement data, the system aims to notify relatives in unexpected situations. It adds an extra layer of safety especially for elderly users or those with chronic conditions.

AI + Sensor Fusion

Highlighted usage scenarios

The scenarios below describe real-life situations and how the system is expected to react, without diving into low-level sensor details.

Scenario 01

Fall / faint detection

When the watch detects a combination of sudden acceleration followed by immobility, it creates a “fall suspicion” event. With location and heart rate / SpO₂ data, an alert can be sent to the responsible team or a predefined contact.

Scenario 02

Fever / temperature change

The temperature sensor is evaluated together with the user’s reference values and environmental conditions. When readings exceed defined thresholds, a warning is shown on the watch and, optionally, a notification can be pushed to the mobile app.

Scenario 03

GPS SOS & geofence

When the user triggers the SOS behavior on the watch or leaves a predefined safe zone, an emergency event is created with GNSS data and sent to the system to be forwarded to the right contacts.

Scenario 04

Heart rate / status tracking

Heart rate, SpO₂ and movement data are monitored together to detect high exertion, panic or abnormal behavior patterns. Instead of a single static threshold, the system focuses on changes over time when generating alerts.

Roadmap

Hardware & system development steps

The project is planned as an iterative process: first a field-testable prototype, then a more permanent system deployment.

Step 01

PCB v1 – Basic sensor prototype

The first board focuses on core motion, heart rate and temperature measurements to build the backbone of “event detection”. The goal is to reach a testable enclosure and firmware in the shortest time.

Step 02

PCB v2 – GNSS-enabled version

A GPS / GNSS module is added to enable geofence, route tracking and SOS scenarios, while optimizing power consumption and enclosure conditions at the same time.

Step 03

Final prototype – Pilot deployment

With IP67-level enclosure, magnetic charging dock and management software, the goal is to deploy the complete system to a pilot site. Feedback then guides the v3 design cycle.
