Scenarios · Demo Flows

When does LifeGuard AI step in?

The flows below describe how the LifeGuard AI SmartHealth Watch behaves in potentially risky situations. Details may change as the real product is developed.

Scenario 1

Fall & fainting suspicion

The user suddenly loses balance and falls at home, outside, or at work and remains motionless for a certain period of time.

Watch: Detects sudden acceleration + strong impact + subsequent long-term inactivity.
LifeGuard AI: Triggers the “fall suspicion” scenario and applies a short observation window to reduce false alarms.
Panel: The related person is highlighted with priority in the family app; a notification is sent to relatives together with location data.
Scenario 2

Prolonged inactivity & risky situation

For elderly people, people living alone or those with chronic conditions, staying inactive for a long time can pose a potential risk.

Watch: Monitors very low movement and staying in the same position for a defined period.
LifeGuard AI: When the “inactivity risk” threshold is exceeded, it produces an alert and flags the situation as a possible risk.
Panel: An “inactivity alert” is shown in the relatives’ panel / mobile app; they can call the user or go to check on them if needed.
Scenario 3

Fever / overall condition deterioration

If the user’s body temperature rises above the normal range or shows a rapid increase in a short time, the system interprets this as a possible fever.

Watch: Regularly tracks skin temperature, heart rate and overall activity data.
LifeGuard AI: When thresholds are exceeded, it creates a “fever early warning” and logs the situation.
Panel: An alert is sent to relatives or an authorized health follower together with the user’s information; a note can be kept for visiting a doctor if necessary.
Scenario 4

Out of GPS zone & risk of getting lost

When a child, elderly person or a user under special follow-up leaves the predefined safe area, the system detects this situation.

Watch: Updates the location information when the user goes outside the boundaries of the defined safe zone.
LifeGuard AI: Calculates the “out-of-zone” probability and performs a short check period to reduce false alarms.
Panel: An “out-of-zone alert” appears in the family app; the last position is shown on the map and the necessary action (calling, messaging, going to the location, etc.) can be planned.
